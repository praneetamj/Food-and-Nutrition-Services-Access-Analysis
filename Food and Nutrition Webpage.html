Executive Summary

This interactive dashboard provides a geospatial and categorical overview of food and nutrition services across Washington, D.C. Using a combination of map-based visualizations, pie charts, and bar graphs, the dashboard highlights the distribution of services by location, category, ward, ZIP code, and census tract.

Key insights include:

  • Most services are concentrated in Wards 4, 5, and 7, with notable density in neighborhoods with higher community needs.
  • Food delivery is the most common service category, followed by grocery distribution and congregate meals.
  • ZIP code 20011 and 20019 have the broadest range of services offered, as visualized in the service treemap.
  • Census tract analysis reveals service clusters, useful for identifying underserved areas.
  • The density heatmap further emphasizes regions of intense service activity, guiding strategic planning.
Together, these insights aim to support stakeholders in optimizing resource allocation and improving food access equity in the district.
